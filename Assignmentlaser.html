Assignment description

We had two assignments:
1- Team assignment where we asked to characterize the laser cutter we have in the lab and make a test parts that vary cutting settings and dimensions
2- Individual assignment in which we should design a parametric press-fit construction kit according to the laser cutter kerf which could be assembled in multiple ways

Learning objectives

1.Demonstrate and describe parametric 2D modelling processes.
2.Identify and explain the processes involved in using the laser cutter.

Introduction

This assignment was very interesting so far as it is the first time we interact with a machine which is the laser cutter.
It is the first time I have ever used such a machine so it was a whole lot of new experience for me.
I will try as much as I can to explain what I have learned so far, all the steps I have done and what issues had appeared when we worked with the machine.

The Steps

Step 1: be familiar with the concepts of laser cutting

Our mentor Ahmed Mansor explained for us the theoretical concept of how the laser cutter works with the machine we have in the lab. If you don't have any background on how the laser cutter works i think you should watch that Video


Step 2: learn rdworks

Rdworks is a CAM tool with provide a G-code for the laser cutter to operate. It's not only a CAM tool it has also alot of CAD tools capabilities so that if you want to have some minor changes in the main design on the final sheet you can make those changes. From the rdworks you can setup your machining settings such as power and speed of cutting.

Laser Cutter characterisation:

Focus: 5mm
Every machine has a focus and this depends on the Lens that collect laser before going on the bed .. in this machine The Lens Focal Point was in 5 mm.

How to know the focus distance? By sending pulses from different z Levels. Until you get the smallest burning point then your focus is ready.
